# CHANNELS.md

## Why These Five Channels Belong Under One Umbrella
These five channels form a coherent commercial intelligence stack around **capital allocation, supply chain decisions, operating risk, and strategic transactions**. Together they answer a single executive question set: *where should we invest, source, expand, hedge, or acquire next?*

Shared umbrella benefits:
- common entity graph (companies, facilities, sectors, geographies, agencies),
- shared change signals (policy, trade, labor, finance, transaction activity),
- cross-channel causality (e.g., tariffs influence sourcing, which influences supplier shifts, which influences M&A strategy),
- unified editorial operations and shared lead pipeline.

---

## Shared Editorial Skeleton
All channel bulletins should follow a common structure for consistency and production efficiency.

1. **Top Line (5 bullets max)**
   - Highest-impact verified updates since last issue.
2. **What Changed This Period**
   - Structured list of `ChangeEvent` clusters.
3. **Why It Matters (Operator Lens)**
   - Practical implications for target audience decisions.
4. **Data Snapshot**
   - Deterministic metrics/tables tied to source records.
5. **Watchlist (Next 1–4 Weeks)**
   - Expected near-term developments and monitoring focus.
6. **Custom Work CTA**
   - Channel-specific consulting/analysis offer.

Common style rules:
- no claims without source-linked references,
- numeric values only from deterministic feature layer,
- concise prose optimized for scanning.

---

## Cross-Channel Overlap
Shared data/model logic across all channels:
- **Entity primitives**: company, government body, geography, sector, product category, facility.
- **Event primitives**: policy update, funding notice, trade rule change, hiring shift, transaction signal.
- **Change significance**: magnitude, novelty, breadth of impact, time sensitivity.
- **Editorial ranking factors**: urgency, strategic relevance, confidence, cross-channel spillover.
- **Contact intent model**: advisory request type, geography, sector, timeline, budget range.

This overlap should be implemented once in shared modules and parameterized per channel.

## 1) Grant / Funding Intelligence

### Target audience
- Economic development teams
- Corporate strategy and public funding teams
- Consultants supporting grant capture
- Innovation program managers

### Why this channel exists
Funding landscapes are fragmented and time-sensitive. Organizations need actionable visibility into new opportunities, deadline shifts, eligibility changes, and award patterns.

### Sample source categories
- Federal/state/local grant portals
- Agency program announcements
- Development bank notices
- Public budget releases
- Awardee disclosure datasets

### Update cadence
- Source checks: daily (or higher for critical portals)
- Bulletin: weekly baseline; twice weekly during high-cycle periods

### Likely entities tracked
- Grant program
- Funding agency
- Applicant/awardee organization
- Region/jurisdiction
- Funding instrument

### Likely geographies / sectors
- Primary: U.S. federal + state
- Expandable: selected international development sources
- Sectors: manufacturing, clean energy, workforce, infrastructure, R&D

### Core outputs
- New/updated funding opportunities
- Deadline/eligibility changes
- Award trend summaries by sector/region
- Program comparison pages

### Sample bulletin sections
- New opportunities this week
- Deadline and eligibility changes
- Notable awards and funding concentration
- Program watchlist (upcoming announcements)

### What makes this channel valuable
It compresses grant scanning and qualification effort, reducing missed opportunities and accelerating submission planning.

### Custom work CTA
“Need a targeted funding map by sector, geography, and project type? Request a custom grant opportunity brief.”

### Future expansion possibilities
- Eligibility pre-screen scoring
- Historical win-rate analytics by applicant profile
- Integration with proposal planning workflows

## 2) Trade / Tariff / Sourcing Intelligence

### Target audience
- Procurement leaders
- Supply chain strategy teams
- Trade compliance teams
- CFO/COO offices managing cost exposure

### Why this channel exists
Trade rules and tariffs can materially shift landed cost and supplier viability. Teams need early visibility into policy shifts and sourcing implications.

### Sample source categories
- Customs/tariff schedules
- Trade ministry notices
- Regulatory bulletins
- Sanctions and restricted-party updates
- Port and logistics advisories

### Update cadence
- Source checks: multiple times per day for high-volatility sources
- Bulletin: weekly baseline; twice weekly during policy-active windows

### Likely entities tracked
- Product code/category
- Country of origin/destination
- Duty/tariff measure
- Supplier/exporter/importer entities
- Regulatory body

### Likely geographies / sectors
- North America, EU, major APAC trade corridors
- Sectors: industrial components, electronics, chemicals, machinery, consumer goods

### Core outputs
- Tariff/regulatory change alerts
- Sourcing risk maps by corridor/category
- Cost-impact scenario summaries
- Compliance watchlists

### Sample bulletin sections
- Policy changes with immediate sourcing impact
- Corridor risk score movements
- Product-category exposure highlights
- Actions to consider this week

### What makes this channel valuable
It translates legal/regulatory noise into direct sourcing and cost implications for operators.

### Custom work CTA
“Need a custom tariff exposure and sourcing alternative analysis for your product mix? Submit your request.”

### Future expansion possibilities
- Landed-cost simulation modules
- Supplier switching recommendation engine (deterministic core)
- Customs delay forecasting overlays

### Sample source categories
- Supplier directories and certifications
- Facility announcements (openings, expansions, closures)
- Industry association updates
- Environmental/permit filings
- Company operational disclosures

### Update cadence
- Source checks: daily
- Bulletin: weekly

### Likely entities tracked
- Supplier company
- Manufacturing facility/site
- Capability/certification
- Production category
- Region/industrial cluster

### Likely geographies / sectors
- U.S. manufacturing regions first
- Expand to North America/EU
- Sectors: automotive, aerospace, electronics, medical devices, industrial equipment

### Core outputs
- Supplier ecosystem changes
- Facility lifecycle events
- Capability availability maps
- Concentration and dependency signals

### Sample bulletin sections
- New entrants and exits
- Capacity expansions and bottlenecks
- Certification and compliance movements
- Cluster-level trend signals

### What makes this channel valuable
It reduces blind spots in supplier networks and supports proactive supplier strategy instead of reactive firefighting.

### Custom work CTA
“Need a supplier landscape and capability gap map for a target component or region? Request custom analysis.”

### Future expansion possibilities
- Tiered supplier graph mapping
- Resilience scoring (single-source risk)
- Make-vs-buy benchmarking views

- Regional economic development indicators

- Industry sector
- Labor market indicator
- Wage/unemployment metrics
- Demand signal category

### Likely geographies / sectors
- U.S. national + state + selected metro views
- Sector overlays for manufacturing, logistics, tech-enabled services

### Core outputs
- Signal dashboards by geography/sector
- Inflection detection (acceleration/deceleration)
- Labor tightness summaries
- Demand and cost pressure watchlists

### Sample bulletin sections
- This week’s market inflections
- Labor availability and wage pressure changes
- Regional operating condition score moves
- Forward watch indicators

### What makes this channel valuable
It filters noisy macro/labor data into operationally relevant decision signals.

### Custom work CTA
“Need a custom regional operating-environment brief for expansion or consolidation decisions? Contact us.”

### Future expansion possibilities

- Leading-indicator composite indexes
- Sector-specific early warning alerts

## 5) M&A / Business Assessment Intelligence

### Target audience
- Corporate development teams
- Private equity and investment teams
- Commercial diligence teams
- Executive leadership evaluating strategic transactions

### Why this channel exists
Transaction opportunity and risk signals are dispersed across filings, ownership changes, operating indicators, and market shifts. Centralized intelligence speeds screening and assessment.

### Sample source categories
- Corporate filings and disclosures
- Transaction announcements/databases
- Ownership and director/officer changes
- Distress/legal event signals
- Sector valuation and deal activity feeds

### Update cadence
- Source checks: daily
- Bulletin: weekly; twice weekly during active deal cycles

### Likely entities tracked
- Target/acquirer company
- Owner/investor entities
- Transaction type/stage
- Financial/operational signal objects
- Advisory ecosystem participants

### Likely geographies / sectors
- U.S. middle-market first
- Sector emphasis: industrials, manufacturing-adjacent services, logistics, tech-enabled B2B

### Core outputs
- Candidate transaction watchlists
- Business health and transition signals
- Deal environment snapshots by sector
- Comparative assessment summaries

### Sample bulletin sections
- New strategic transaction signals
- Business assessment red/yellow/green movements
- Sector deal velocity indicators
- Near-term diligence watchlist

### What makes this channel valuable
It shortens time-to-insight for opportunity screening and surfaces risks earlier in diligence cycles.

### Custom work CTA
“Need a custom target-screening or business assessment brief for a specific sector/theme? Submit an engagement request.”

### Future expansion possibilities
- Scoring model for acquisition attractiveness (deterministic + editorial overlay)
- Integration with proprietary target lists
- Post-acquisition monitoring streams
